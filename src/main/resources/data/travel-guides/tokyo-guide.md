# Tokyo Travel Guide

## Overview

Tokyo, Japan's bustling capital, seamlessly blends ultramodern technology with traditional temples. The city offers an incredible mix of ancient shrines, cutting-edge architecture, world-class cuisine, and vibrant pop culture.

## Best Time to Visit

- **Spring (March-May)**: Cherry blossom season, mild weather, peak tourist time
- **Autumn (September-November)**: Beautiful fall foliage, comfortable temperatures
- **Summer (June-August)**: Hot and humid, but lively festivals
- **Winter (December-February)**: Cold but less crowded, excellent for hot springs

## Top Attractions

### Senso-ji Temple
Tokyo's oldest Buddhist temple, located in Asakusa. The iconic Kaminarimon (Thunder Gate) with its massive red lantern is a must-see. The surrounding Nakamise shopping street offers traditional snacks and souvenirs.

### Shibuya Crossing
The world's busiest pedestrian crossing. Best viewed from the Starbucks overlooking the intersection or the Shibuya Sky observation deck.

### Meiji Shrine
A serene Shinto shrine dedicated to Emperor Meiji, set in a peaceful forest in the heart of the city. Perfect for experiencing traditional Japanese spirituality.

### Tokyo Skytree
At 634 meters, it's the tallest tower in Japan. The observation decks offer panoramic views of the entire Tokyo metropolitan area.

### Tsukiji Outer Market
While the inner wholesale market moved to Toyosu, the outer market remains a food lover's paradise with fresh sushi, tamagoyaki, and Japanese street food.

## Neighborhoods to Explore

### Shinjuku
The entertainment and business hub with towering skyscrapers, the famous Robot Restaurant, and the atmospheric Golden Gai bar district.

### Harajuku
Youth fashion capital featuring Takeshita Street's quirky shops, vintage stores, and creative fashion subcultures.

### Akihabara
Electronics and anime paradise. Multi-story arcades, manga shops, and maid cafes define this otaku (geek) culture center.

### Ginza
Upscale shopping district with luxury boutiques, department stores, and high-end dining establishments.

## Food Recommendations

- **Ramen**: Try Ichiran for tonkotsu or Fuunji for tsukemen
- **Sushi**: Conveyor belt sushi at Genki Sushi or omakase at high-end establishments
- **Tempura**: Light, crispy battered vegetables and seafood
- **Izakaya**: Japanese pub-style dining with small plates
- **Wagyu Beef**: Premium Japanese beef at yakiniku restaurants

## Transportation Tips

- Get a **Suica or Pasmo card** for convenient train and subway travel
- The **JR Yamanote Line** circles central Tokyo and connects major stations
- Consider a **JR Pass** if visiting other cities in Japan
- Taxis are expensive but convenient for late-night travel

## Budget Estimates

- Budget traveler: $80-100/day (hostels, convenience store meals)
- Mid-range: $150-250/day (business hotels, casual restaurants)
- Luxury: $400+/day (premium hotels, fine dining)

## Cultural Tips

- Bow when greeting people
- Remove shoes when entering homes and some restaurants
- Don't tip - it's not customary and can be considered rude
- Speak quietly on public transportation
- Cash is still widely preferred over cards
