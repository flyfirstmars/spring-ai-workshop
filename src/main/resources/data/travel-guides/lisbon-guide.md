# Lisbon Travel Guide

## Overview

Lisbon, Portugal's hilly coastal capital, is one of Europe's most charismatic cities. Known for its pastel-colored buildings, historic trams, melancholic fado music, and delicious pastries, Lisbon offers a perfect blend of old-world charm and contemporary culture.

## Best Time to Visit

- **Spring (April-June)**: Pleasant weather, fewer crowds, blooming gardens
- **Summer (July-August)**: Hot and crowded, but vibrant festivals
- **Autumn (September-October)**: Warm weather, wine harvest season
- **Winter (November-March)**: Mild but rainy, excellent for budget travelers

## Top Attractions

### Belem Tower
A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this 16th-century fortified tower is an iconic symbol of Portugal's Age of Discoveries. Located on the Tagus River waterfront.

### Jeronimos Monastery
A masterpiece of Manueline architecture housing the tomb of explorer Vasco da Gama. The intricate stonework and cloisters are breathtaking.

### Alfama District
Lisbon's oldest neighborhood with narrow cobblestone streets, traditional fado houses, and the impressive Sao Jorge Castle offering panoramic city views.

### Tram 28
The iconic yellow vintage tram winds through Lisbon's historic neighborhoods. A tourist attraction in itself, offering a scenic journey through the city's hills.

### LX Factory
A creative hub in a converted industrial complex featuring independent shops, restaurants, bookstores, and weekend markets.

## Neighborhoods to Explore

### Baixa
The downtown area rebuilt after the 1755 earthquake with elegant squares, pedestrian streets, and traditional shops.

### Chiado
The bohemian quarter with theaters, bookshops, and historic cafes like A Brasileira where poet Fernando Pessoa once sat.

### Bairro Alto
The nightlife district with countless bars and restaurants. Quiet by day, bustling after dark.

### Principe Real
Trendy neighborhood with designer boutiques, antique shops, and beautiful gardens.

## Food Recommendations

### Must-Try Dishes
- **Pastel de Nata**: Iconic custard tarts, best at Pasteis de Belem
- **Bacalhau**: Salt cod prepared in countless ways (365 recipes, they say)
- **Sardinhas Assadas**: Grilled sardines, especially during June festivals
- **Bifana**: Pork sandwich in a crusty roll
- **Francesinha**: Porto specialty - a hearty meat sandwich with cheese and beer sauce

### Drinks
- **Ginjinha**: Cherry liqueur served in chocolate cups
- **Vinho Verde**: Refreshing young green wine
- **Portuguese wines**: Explore Douro, Alentejo, and Dao regions

## Transportation Tips

- Walking is the best way to explore the historic center
- **Lisboa Card** offers free public transport and museum discounts
- Metro system is clean, efficient, and affordable
- Uber and Bolt are widely available
- Be prepared for hills - comfortable shoes are essential

## Budget Estimates

- Budget traveler: EUR 50-70/day (hostels, local eateries)
- Mid-range: EUR 100-150/day (boutique hotels, nice restaurants)
- Luxury: EUR 250+/day (5-star hotels, fine dining)

## Day Trips

### Sintra
Fairy-tale palaces and castles nestled in forested hills. The colorful Pena Palace is unmissable. 40 minutes by train.

### Cascais
Charming coastal town with beautiful beaches and seafood restaurants. 30 minutes by train.

### Obidos
Medieval walled town famous for its ginjinha served in chocolate cups. Great for a romantic afternoon.

## Cultural Tips

- Lunch is typically 12:30-3pm, dinner after 8pm
- Many locals speak English, but learning basic Portuguese is appreciated
- Tipping 5-10% is customary but not mandatory
- Sunday is family day - some shops close
- Lisbon is generally very safe, but watch for pickpockets in tourist areas
